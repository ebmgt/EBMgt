Four Assertions

1. Creating workforce a workforce psychological engagement and achieving organizational performance are not a zero-sum scenario, but can be synergistic.

  • Measured by: UWES 3 and other scales
  • Antecedents: Self-determination theory (SDT) (Ryan & Deci) and Engagement framework(Kahn, 1990)

    2. Creating workforce engagement is necessary...
    ...but not sufficient to improve organizational performance.
    • Engagement may have a nonlinear relationship with achieving mission outcomes (1)
    • Complexity science, implemented by various approaches, can design the gathering and organizing the information needed to guide an engaged workforce to innovation and improvement. (2)

      Leadership approaches consistent with complexity science include Uhl-Bien's “Enabling Leadership” (aka “Complexity leadership theory”), Marquet’s “Intent-based leadership”, McChrystal‘s “Gardener Leadership”, positive deviance, agile, and others.

    Additional implications

    Measurement of burnout is not well standardized (1). We need to revise management development as current leader education may focus on promoting the manager more than the organization (Straus et al, 2)).
    In addition to rethinking management with the methods above, QI methods should be revised as its current basis on outdated modes of management may contribute to burnout (3).
    While teaching and disseminating new management tactics is needed, “leadership-light” tactics can be taught to all of the workforce (4).     		What we have found:
